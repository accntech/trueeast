# TRUE EAST ENERGY CORP. — Project Brief

Company profile website for TRUE EAST ENERGY CORP., a Philippine solar power
company (SEC 202406015446-02) based in Bongabong, Oriental Mindoro. Engaged in
power generation, transmission and distribution; born out of rice-mill
operations where high electricity costs forced a move to solar. Led by
Engr. Vincent Vir M. Agbayani, Registered Electrical Engineer (Mapúa), 14 yrs
plant-engineering experience. Delivers turn-key On-Grid and Hybrid (battery)
solar. PhilGEPS-registered for government projects. 17 completed projects
(~550 kWp), 2 major ongoing (LGU Roxas 100 kWp, Pinamalayan Doctors Hospital
150 kWp).

## Design Context

### Users
Provincial Filipino business owners (rice mills, hotels, clinics, gas stations,
shops), LGU / hospital procurement officers, parishes and schools, and
homeowners across Oriental Mindoro researching solar to cut electricity bills.
They browse mostly on phones, in daylight, often on patchy mobile data. The job:
quickly trust that this is a competent, real, locally-proven engineering firm —
and see proof (real installations, real capacities, real client types) — before
sending an inquiry.

### Brand Personality
Sturdy · sun-bleached · electrical. Grounded and proven, not flashy.
An engineer's precision with provincial warmth. Confident because the work
speaks for itself — drone shots of real arrays on real tin roofs amid rice
fields. Emotion to evoke: earned trust and quiet optimism ("power that rises
in the East").

### Aesthetic Direction
Cinematic solar editorial. LIGHT theme — warm, sun-bleached paper, like a
field equipment manual left in tropical sun — deliberately rejecting the AI/
cliché "dark solar-tech site with glowing cyan accents." Dark appears ONLY as
full-bleed cinematic photo interludes (the aerial array shots), for editorial
rhythm. Real photography is the hero, treated like a magazine photo essay.
Anti-references: SaaS dark dashboards, neon gradients, glassmorphism, generic
agency template grids.

Type: Big Shoulders (industrial condensed display — power-infrastructure /
provincial signboard voice) + Hanken Grotesk (humane neutral body). Two
families only; labels are tracked uppercase Hanken/Big Shoulders, never a
mono-as-technical reflex.

Color: OKLCH, neutrals tinted toward solar-amber. Solar-amber (the rising
sun — "True East") is the rare 10% punch on CTAs and the signature sunrise
rule; panel-green (echoing the literal green roofs in the photos) carries
structure. No pure black/white. No gradient text. No side-stripe cards.

### Design Principles
1. Photography first — real installations carry the credibility; UI frames
   them, never competes.
2. Earned, not asserted — lead with verifiable numbers and named projects;
   restraint over superlatives.
3. Sun-bleached, not dark — daylight palette is the differentiator; dark is a
   photographic interlude, not the theme.
4. Engineer's rhythm — precise spacing scale, tracked labels, tabular figures;
   one bold condensed voice for headlines.
5. High-impact motion only — one orchestrated load reveal + scroll clip-path
   reveals; transform/opacity only; honor prefers-reduced-motion.
